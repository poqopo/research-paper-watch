## Candidate subtopics to consider

These are useful next branches, but they are not active graph nodes yet.

- Structural variants and long-read genomics: methods and clinical studies for SVs, repeat expansions, hard-to-map loci, phased haplotypes, and long-read rare-disease diagnosis.
- Noncoding and regulatory variants: prioritization of promoters, enhancers, UTRs, deep intronic variants, and regulatory pathogenic mechanisms.
- Splicing and RNA diagnostics: RNA-seq, splice prediction, aberrant expression, allele-specific expression, and transcriptome-based diagnosis.
- Deep phenotyping and phenotype extraction: HPO extraction, clinical NLP, facial/gestalt matching, EHR-based phenotyping, and phenotype quality control.
- Reanalysis and data sharing: periodic reanalysis, Matchmaker-style exchange, federated rare-disease cohorts, and diagnosis after negative first-pass testing.
- Functional validation: model organisms, CRISPR screens, cellular assays, organoids, and experimental evidence for candidate genes or variants.
- Newborn, prenatal, and rapid diagnosis: NICU/PICU rapid sequencing, prenatal diagnosis, turnaround-time studies, and implementation outcomes.
- Therapeutic actionability: papers that connect diagnosis to treatment, surveillance, trial matching, or management changes.
