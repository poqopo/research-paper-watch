# Enhancing Cross-Context Generalization in Drug Perturbation Prediction with a Multimodal Conditional Diffusion Framework

## Healthcare relevance

Improves prediction of drug-induced transcriptional responses across drugs and cellular contexts.

## Analysis

- Abstract: MOTIVATION: Predicting drug-induced transcriptional perturbations is critical for precision medicine, yet existing models fail to capture multimodal biological context, limiting generalization across unseen drugs and cell lines.  RESULTS: We present PertDiff, a conditional diffusion framework that integrates control gene expression, LLM-derived cell semantics, and pretrained molecular graph representations to predict transcriptome-wide perturbations.  PertDiff outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in prediction accuracy and generalizes robustly across drugs and cell lines.
