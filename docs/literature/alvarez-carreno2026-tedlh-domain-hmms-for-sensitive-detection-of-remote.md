# TEDLH: Domain HMMs for sensitive detection of remote homologues

## Healthcare relevance

Improves functional interpretation of proteins and remote homologs, which can support variant and target annotation.

## Analysis

- Abstract: MOTIVATION: The Encyclopedia of Domains (TED) provides domain annotations for proteins in the AlphaFold Protein Structure Database (AFDB) using a consensus of three state-of-the-art structure-based methods.  We used these annotations to construct profile Hidden Markov models (HMMs), collectively forming the TED Library of HMMs (TEDLH).  TEDLH enables sensitive sequence and profile searches, supporting systematic exploration of protein domain families and their evolutionary relationships.
- Prior limitation: The Encyclopedia of Domains (TED) provides domain annotations for proteins in the AlphaFold Protein Structure Database (AFDB) using a consensus of three state-of-the-art structure-based methods.  We used these annotations to construct profile Hidden Markov models (HMMs), collectively forming the TED Library of HMMs (TEDLH).
- Differentiator: TEDLH links 934,186 domain HMMs to experimentally determined CATH-PDB structures through direct (primary) and transitive (secondary and tertiary) relationships.  Fewer than half of TEDLH HMMs are directly linked to a CATH-PDB domain; the remaining models are connected through transitive relationships.
