# Inferring Dynamic Information from Protein Structures by Gaussian Integrals and Deep Learning

## Healthcare relevance

Improves scalable inference of protein dynamics from static structures.

## Analysis

- Abstract: MOTIVATION: Protein dynamics are central to function, but experiments and molecular dynamics (MD) simulations remain costly, low-throughput, and difficult to compare across protocols.  Scalable structure-based methods are needed to infer dynamics from static protein structures.  RESULTS: We present a deep learning framework that predicts protein dynamics from 30-dimensional Gaussian integral (GI) descriptors of Calpha backbone topology.
- Prior limitation: Protein dynamics are central to function, but experiments and molecular dynamics (MD) simulations remain costly, low-throughput, and difficult to compare across protocols.  Scalable structure-based methods are needed to infer dynamics from static protein structures.
- Differentiator: We present a deep learning framework that predicts protein dynamics from 30-dimensional Gaussian integral (GI) descriptors of Calpha backbone topology.  Using 1,374 ATLAS protein chains with MD-derived RMSF, GI stratified proteins into fold-relevant clusters enriched for secondary structure, sequence homology, and ECOD families.
