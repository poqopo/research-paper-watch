# UK Biobank whole-genome sequencing reveals robust contributions of rare variants to complex-trait heritability.

## Healthcare relevance

Clarifies how much rare variants contribute to complex traits and supports sequencing-based risk architecture studies.

## Analysis

- Abstract: BACKGROUND: The missing heritability of complex traits remains a key challenge in human genetics.  Recent studies have highlighted the potential role of rare variants in addressing this gap.  However, their contribution has been difficult to quantify due to small sample sizes and large standard errors in large-scale whole-genome sequencing data.  RESULTS: Leveraging whole-genome sequencing data from 348,977 unrelated White British participants in the UK Biobank, we analyze 5,378,300 linkage disequilibrium-independent rare variants to estimate their heritability for five complex traits.
- Prior limitation: Rare-variant contributions to complex-trait heritability were hard to quantify because earlier WGS cohorts had small sample sizes and large standard errors.
- Differentiator: Uses very large UK Biobank WGS data to estimate trait-specific rare-variant heritability with substantially improved precision.
