# Somatic mobility of transposons is explosive and shaped by distinct integration biases in Arabidopsis thaliana.

## Healthcare relevance

Not directly clinical, but useful for understanding genome instability, epigenetic regulation, and adaptive genome variation in plant systems.

## Analysis

- Abstract: BACKGROUND: The movement of transposable elements (TEs) in somatic cells generates genetic and phenotypic diversity across the soma of individual organisms.  This process is especially important in plants that display a sessile lifestyle, can regenerate from somatic cells, and continuously establish their germline from somatic stem cells.  Yet, the scale and integration biases by which TEs may shape the plant soma remain unknown.  RESULTS: We adapt high-throughput TE sequencing and introduce a new computational pipeline, "deNOVOEnrich", to identify and analyse somatic transposition events in Arabidopsis thaliana.
- Prior limitation: Somatic transposition scale and integration bias in plants had not been measured at genome-wide resolution.
- Differentiator: Shows explosive but structured somatic TE mobility in Arabidopsis and links integration preferences to chromatin context and environmentally responsive genes.
